Wreck It Raplh

Wreck-It Ralph follows Ralph, a video game villain, who wants to prove he's a hero. He ventures through different games and meets Vanellope, a glitchy racer, and together they form an unlikely friendship. As Ralph helps Vanellope, he learns that being a hero isn't about titles but actions. The movie blends humor, heart, and video game nostalgia, with strong voice performances from John C. Reilly, Sarah Silverman, and others. It delivers a message about self-acceptance and personal growth, making it a fun and emotional experience for all ages. I give this movie a ★ 9/10.

Pixels

Pixels is a sci-fi comedy where a group of childhood friends must save Earth from alien invaders who attack using video game-themed creatures. The team, led by arcade champion Sam Brenner (Adam Sandler), battles classic games like Pac-Man and Donkey Kong turned into real-life threats. The film features comedic performances from Sandler, Kevin James, Josh Gad, and Peter Dinklage. While the nostalgic video game references and CGI effects are fun, the humor often feels forced, and the plot is light. It's an entertaining but forgettable action-comedy for fans of 80s gaming culture. I give it a ★6/10

Big Hero 6

Big Hero 6 follows Hiro Hamada, a young robotics genius, who teams up with his late brothers robot, Baymax, to stop a villain using Hiros tech for destruction. Hiro forms a superhero team with his friends to save the city. The film explores themes of grief, healing, and friendship, with a strong emotional focus on the bond between Hiro and Baymax. The voice cast, including Ryan Potter and Scott Adsit, delivers standout performances. Overall, it's a heartwarming, action-packed movie that blends humor, superhero fun, and emotional depth. I give it a ★8/10
